Network and Information Security Management

Collaborative Learning Discussion 1

Compromising a Medical Mannequin, Discuss the major threats and vulnerabilities in the paper, and how to mitigate against these?
Provide a summary of the discussion based on your initial post, the feedback from your peers and the content of all 3 units.

SUMMARY POST

My initial post discussed the major threats and vulnerabilities of a medical mannequin as well as strategies that could be used to mitigate these. The post discusses a real time hack of a medical device and the cyber security vulnerabilities that were perhaps not considered in detail by the health sector previously. A Brute Force attack is very effective as you can set it and leave it to run until you have gained entry (Varonis, 2021). A Denial of Service (DOS) attack is designed to shut down or make the target unusable. 

There were some engaging points made in the discussion, of particular interest to me is Shota’s mention of WPS with Personal Identification Number (PIN). A WPS Pin attack is often overlooked as a security vulnerability but by intercepting the handshake when the button is pressed is relatively easy using a Reaver attack (Outpost, 2020). Whilst the points made regarding WPS security seem particularly valid and could be of great use in many scenarios. Upon further investigation there is no mention of medical devices that use this particular function of initial connectivity. However, securing the Wi-Fi with a more secure protocol like WPA2 or even the newer WPA3 makes the complete sense as this uses the advanced encryption standard (Panda Security, 2020).

Beran makes mention of a Defence In Depth (DID) strategy, an effective control in protecting ones environment and assets. According to the Center for Internet security (CIS, 2021) this strategy involves multiple layers of security controls and mechanisms designed to protect the confidentiality, integrity and availability of the date on the network. If DID is implemented successfully this type of layered protection is effective by improving redundancy in the event one mechanism is compromised (CIS, 2021).

My recommendation of mitigation through proactive system monitoring, setting of all alerts, implementation of VPN’s with the added requirement of MFA still stands. However as mentioned above implementing strong Wi-Fi encryption protocols and using a DID approach can also be a highly effective means of mitigation.

Healthcare has been the most targeted industry for cyber criminals, Tamari (2021) and medical devices have changed from being fully non-connected to now being both wireless and software controlled. Although there is an international standard to which medical devices must certify (this is the IEC 62304 Medical device software and was put in place to prevent cyber-criminal events on medical devices). The standard has not been updated to consider and include either network connectivity or cybersecurity (Williams, Woolward, 2015). When medical device manufacturers are required by law to implement strict security measures into the life cycle of their products, we will likely see the aforementioned threats and vulnerabilities decrease.
