Dissertation Writing

A major requirement for an award of a Masters’ degree and an undergraduate degree is a dissertation thesis. Many universities still have a tradition that following the submission of a dissertation, students must attend an oral examination where they defend their findings in front of an academic committee.  For the Computing programmes, you will be required to present an artefact as part of your project submission. The two parts of the dissertation submission cover 2 key skills:

- Written Presentation skills
- Oral Presentation skills

How to Get Your Research Published

As you are only allowed one submission at a time, target a realistic journal first. Check the website of your target journal for specific guidance on your manuscript and stick with it. For example, if they would like an introduction to contain 3 paragraphs do not be tempted to write 4!

Strictly follow all the other guidance and requirements to minimise the risk of rejection. If that happens don’t be offended, very few papers get through the process smoothly, with the anecdotal evidence suggesting the usual time from submission to publication now being over a year.

Remember that if you get rejected you can start a submission to the next journal on your list. In the meantime, read the articles from this journal regularly. Thanks to this you will be able to understand what the editors’ want, and what the style of that journal is. Look at your results and the findings of your study. Is it interesting? How are your findings supported?

Please remember that journal publications, although important and desirable, are not the only ways of disseminating your work. You should regularly check the call for abstracts for upcoming conferences in your field. You can also use your work in organisational reports and newsletters. Another way to disseminate your work would be to prepare and deliver a seminar for fellow colleagues!

RESEARCH PROPOSAL PRESENTATION

Assignment Brief

The research proposal presentation fulfils the learning outcomes where you will evaluate critically existing literature, research design and methodology for your chosen topic, including data analysis processes and so produce and evaluate critically a research proposal for your chosen topic.

Topic:
Determine the different techniques to reduce cyberbullying and evaluate their effectiveness.

You are required to create an audio-narrated slide presentation and a transcript in which you will present the following sections. These sections are required in a standard research proposal for the Computing department. You are free to combine/merge sections, where appropriate and without detriment to the structure of your presentation.

- Project Title.
- Significance/Contribution to the discipline/Research Problem.
- Research Question.
- Aims and Objectives.
- Key literature related to the project.
- Methodology/Development strategy/Research Design.
- Ethical considerations and risk assessment (as part of your ethical approval application).
- Description of artefact(s) that will be created (if applicable).
- Timeline of proposed activities.
