Module 8 - Unit 2

Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal


Week 2 where you will be introduced to the various aspects of a research proposal, starting with defining a research question.

Defining the topic of your research may be a tedious process but it is essential to ensure you are successful in your research. You will start with an initial research idea and then ask pertinent questions that may need further revisions before you prepare your research proposal, which is the foundation to your research.

One element of the proposal is a literature review within your chosen area. A literature review is a survey of scholarly sources on a specific topic. It provides an overview of current knowledge, allowing you to identify relevant theories, methods, and gaps in the existing research. In this unit, you will be introduced to the process of completing a literature review and how to present your review. This will be based on a list of topics, from which you will choose one that interests you the most.

On completion of this unit you will be able to:

- Examine the characteristics that make up a suitable research topic.
- Explore rational and creative methods for formulating a research idea.
- Identify means of transforming research ideas into crafted research questions and proposal.
- Conduct a literature search, critique the literature and to present a literature review.

Richard Meadows
Initial Post

Corazón’s implantable heart monitoring device comes with several issues that can run into ethical conflicts. First, the issue of physical invasiveness, and then of data privacy. The device is literally inside someone’s body, this is about as invasive as you can get. Additionally, it must be able to protect the privacy of the patient data it gathers. The Medical Implant Risk Analysis case study does mention it employs cryptographic algorithms to secure the data at rest, and also when transmitting. Therefore, the business is clearly trying to adhere to ACM’s Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct (the Code)’s principles of respecting privacy 1.6 and honouring confidentiality 1.7 (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018).

There is the possibility of human error in implementation, maintenance, and monitoring, and Corazón’s “vocal” commitment fosters ambiguity, thus leaving them at risk of violating principle 1.2, and also the Belmont Report’s Beneficence principle (U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, 1979).

It also offers its services to those who would otherwise be unable to access it, using charities as a vehicle, thus considering BCS’s Code of Conduct, Public Interest principle 4 (British Computer Society, N.D.).

Running a bug bounty program is a very positive ethical step as it shows Corazón cares about patients enough to introduce such proactive security measures to protect their privacy. Short range communication is also a good choice to protect this. These actions adhere to ACM’s Code principle 2.5, and 2.6 is adhered to by default (Association for Computing Machinery, 2018).

Corazón additionally adheres to several other BCS Code of Ethics’ principles, including Professional Competence and Integrity, principles 1, 4, and 6 (British Computer Society, N.D.).
