DEVELOPMENT TEAM PROJECT
by Mathew van Beek

A DESIGN DOCUMENT/PROPOSAL OF A NETWORK AND INFORMATION SECURITY MANAGEMENT (NISM) ASSESSMENT PROJECT

Purpose of assessment

A vulnerability assessment is way of identifying security vulnerabilities. An assessment of security procedures which may indicate potential weaknesses and provide appropriate mitigation procedures (Rahalkar, 2018). By utilizing Center for Internet Security (CIS) and International Organization for Standardization (ISO) controls we would aim to reduce and ultimately eliminate weaknesses to an acceptable risk level based on the enterprises risk appetite.

Method of assessment

A combination of CIS and ISO will be used for vulnerability identification and tailored to the enterprise’s organization or purpose. CIS is an information security risk assessment method that aids enterprises in implementing and assessing their security posture against the CIS controls cybersecurity best practices. CIS methodology is applied to assess configuration of workstations, firewalls, and network devices (CIS.org, 2021). ISO methodology involves implementing a risk assessment that can be frequently reviewed and enhanced to assess and correct, identifying the activities for which standards are used. Once completed one can investigate how these activities, supported using standards, contribute to the creation of value for the enterprise, ISO 27,001 is a standard outlining how any business should handle risk at every level (ISO.org, 2013).

Tools to be used in this assessment

1. Nmap: Used to map the networks attack surface, this tool scans for hosts, operating systems, hardware versions, software versions and open port vulnerabilities (Nmap.org 2020).
2. Nessus Vulnerability scanner in conjunction with Megasploit: Nessus as a tool checks all computers, servers, and firewalls for open ports and potentially vulnerable software. Megasploit is used to confirm whether or not the reported holes are exploitable (Oracle.com, 2021).
3. Burp Suite: Used to scan the security of apps, web browsers and web applications. This tool discovers the application function, scans for security holes and launches custom attacks (Kiprin, 2021).

Business impacts relating to the use of these tools and methods

1. Scanning a host with Nmap can take just a fifth of a second, however this time allocation can quickly mount, dependent on the number of hosts in an environment. This scan can however be run during office hours with very little to no impact on the network (Nmap.org 2020).
2. Applications on hosts have been known to crash when a Nessus Vulnerability scanner is taking place, therefore it is recommended to perform a scan of this nature on a production environment after standard working hours, or in an allocated change window. (Tenable.org 2019).
3. Burp Suite should impose no impact on users as this type of scan has been programmed to find backdoors and run in an undetected manner (Ravello, 2016).


Security challenges identified in our subject entities virtual landscape

A. Web apps and staff not available on test days leaving systems vulnerable to failure
B. Lack of executive management support, bureaucratic approach hinders decision making
C. Lacking in organizational understanding and in the process of a risk assessment
D. Lack of communication and technical understanding (Hutchins, 2020)

Limitations and assumptions of this assessment

Our team will need to have access to the systems and infrastructure for the entire duration of the security assessment. We will need to be provided with login account information and the credentials deemed necessary to use in the testing of devices to perform authenticated scans of devices and applications. We will require permission to connect testing laptops to all the entity’s networks. All the entities personnel and business units are expected to cooperate and collaborate with the assessment process. A change freeze will need to be put in place pertaining any infrastructure upgrades or changes for the duration of the security assessment period. Support must be provided when access to the environment is required outside of regular working hours (Rahalkar, 2018).
