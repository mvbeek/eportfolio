Module 3 - Unit 1

Information Risk Management

Lecture on Definitions, Standards and Approaches

Definitions Transcript

Risk is not an easy concept to define – and can have different meanings depending on context and situation. One of the challenges of risk management is ensuring that all parties have a common understanding of risk. Hubbard (2009) spends an entire chapter of his book discussing the various definitions of risk that are used – his preferred definition is given here.

Hubbard starts by defining uncertainty - "Uncertainty .. Is the existence of more than one possibility. The “true” outcome/ state/result/value is not known." He then goes on to define how uncertainty is measured, "Measurement of uncertainty (is by) a set of probabilities assigned to a set of possibilities. For example, “There is a 60% chance it will rain tomorrow, and a 40% chance it won’t.” Finally, he goes on to define risk as "a state of uncertainty where some of the possibilities involve a loss, injury, catastrophe, or other undesirable outcome (i.e., something bad could happen)" (Hubbard, 2009). As mentioned above in chapter 5 of his book Hubbard reviews various risk definitions – a link to an online version is given in the references.

Blakely et al define risk as "the possibility of an event which would reduce the value of the business were it to occur. Such an event is called an 'adverse event' ". They also define a measure of risk as 'annualised loss expectation' (ALE). "ALE is the expected cumulative cost of risk over a period of one year as estimated in advance". (Blakely et al, 2002).

Josey et al state "The Open FAIR Body of Knowledge defines risk as the probable frequency and magnitude of future loss. " (Josey et al, 2014). They go on to say "Risk is a derived value, similar to speed, which is also a derived value – risk equals the probable frequency and probable magnitude of future loss. In formal terms risk is derived from the combination of threat event frequency, vulnerability, and asset value and liability characteristics".

Stoneburner et al (2002) define risk as "the net negative impact of the exercise of a vulnerability, considering both the probability and the impact of occurrence". They also define risk management as "the process of identifying risk, assessing risk, and taking steps to reduce risk to an acceptable level".

Finally, the course text (Sutton, 2016) says "Information risk is a subset of business risk and relates to the confidentiality, integrity and availability of business information assets and the information management infrastructure... the general principles of business risk management still apply".
