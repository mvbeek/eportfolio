Initial post: why companies need to invest in cybersecurity

For companies confronting risks related to cybersecurity, the question is how much money needed to be spent for their data to be secure, measuring such risk is difficult, and budgets for information security cannot be justified through financial tests that are applied to the other investments. For instance, it is possible to find out how much money is stolen from banks each year, but it is impossible to specify how much money is withdrawn electronically in a cybersecurity attack.

One of the major attacks took place a few years ago, the Saudi Arabian Oil Company also known as Aramco, is a public company which operates in the petroleum and natural gas sector, it is one of the biggest companies in the world by revenue. In 2013, a virus generated from an external source attacked the company and approximately 30,000 of its workstations were infected, the company supposes that the virus-infected only the workstations with no harm being done on the main part of the network (Van. B, Pretorius, B, 2015).

Such attacks would drive Aramco and other companies to invest in cybersecurity, as well as protecting the sensitive data, intellectual property, financial information, and trade secrets, as these are solid reasons for companies to consider cybersecurity issues.

Finally, the increased number of breaches and cybersecurity attacks helped companies to be aware of the importance of cybersecurity, mainly for those companies that are utilizing cloud computing services or IoT technology.

PEER RESPONSE

The points you make are very interesting. It would be good to establish if your comments, particularly in your opening statement reflect solely your own opinion or if these points are made with reference to an academic body of writing?

In my professional experience companies tend to avoid investment in cybersecurity until a breach is made and the requirement becomes obvious to them due to substantial losses made. This can either be cash losses, ransomware payment needs, or even simply the paid loss of labor force hours due to technical systems being down.

Corporations are constantly looking for ROI, however, it can be difficult to qualify the quantitative reasons for such a high level of investment when they have yet to experience the damage of a security breach (Gordon, Loeb, Lucyshyn 2014). Once they have experienced a rupture, however, it tends to be much easier to secure the project financing and sign off the labor hours and software required.

Whilst you refer to ‘companies’ in general, it could also be important to consider the size of the corporations that we are referring to. For each institution, it will likely be essential that the cost of the security implementation is not higher than the potential cost of a breach. We must remember that risk is relative. These costs are potentially much more easily absorbed by multinational corporations than small ltd businesses.

Initial Post- Importance of Cyber Security

Cyber Security is not only a direct threat to companies or individual data and resources, but it can also have a significant influence on the image a company has depending if it is protected from cyber threats or not. (Stokdyk, 2021) 

Cyber vulnerabilities can have devastating effects as well as legal ones to companies that don’t do enough to protect themselves from such exposures. If a company is successfully attacked, its losses can usually be just financial ones, or the company image is being affected. Still, when government agencies or strategic country resources are being targeted, this can jeopardise public safety.

This was the case of a pipeline shut down that supplied 45% of the fuel consumed by the East Coast of the United States of America from a group of a criminal ransomware gang called Dark Horse. (CHOI, 2021). The pipeline shutdown has affected 17 states and the District of Columbia, which issued an emergency declaration due to this event which helped with the assistance of the local carriers and petroleum providers. 

Due to the constant vulnerabilities that the open internet provides, companies and government agencies need to invest substantially and smartly into Cyber Security to better protect vital assets and data.

Initial Post: Colonial Pipeline and their out of date IT infrastructure

Cyber Security is nowadays a global issue. The fact that small and large industries still use older systems due to cost of upgrade, Executive laziness and very limited knowledge of the regular user when it comes to Cyber Security awareness, has made this issue even more challenging.

Sometimes between May 6th and 7th, Darkside, the famous Russian hackers group performed a successful Cyber Attack on one of the major American oil pipeline system, Colonial Pipeline, that originates in Houston, Texas and carries fuel to the Southeastern United States (Various News agencies, 2021).

As a result, Colonial Pipeline halted all of their operations, which created a sort of chaos in the airports and huge line-ups in the gas stations.

We do understand that Cyber Security is a huge challenge, but we can limit if not completely close, the hackers access to a network by investing in the following areas:

1- A Solid Disaster Recovery Solution, with this, no matter how hard hit your network is, having a well-implemented Disaster Recovery Solution helps you restore your systems within hours.

2- Cyber Security awareness for everyone, for all types of users, from a simple user to administrators to Executives as everyone play equal and important parts when it comes to securing a network.

3- Human Resources, hire Cyber Security personnel than relying on contractors. A contractor implements security measures for your systems and network, but a Cyber Security personnel in your team, continuously improves the Cyber Security measures, keeps an eye on ongoing network threats and provides end user awareness on time.

4- Lastly, upgrade your systems, both; software and hardware.

Initial Post: WhatsApp - data confidentially for data in transit

Hi, for this discussion I choose the company Facebook, specifically their subsidiary and popular messaging service WhatsApp with over 400 million users (Iqbaal, 2017) . Whatsapp is a text and voice messaging service which uses networks such cellular networks or wifi. With its broad userbase it is especially important that the user feel secure and confident that their messages cannot be intercepted or altered.

I will now examine the messaging service in the context of data confidentially, in particular while sending messages (data in transit)

In the introduction into cyber security lecture the ethical responsibility of cyber security, namely data protection, data confidentiality and privacy were mentioned. Data confidentially is described as minimising the amount of exposed data (Troncoso, 2019). Especially data in transit from one endpoint to another via a network like the internet is at risk of being exposed and having its confidentially compromised. To send data via networks is the core service of WhatsApp. Interception of the data, often called man-in-the-middle attack, can change the content of the data package before it reaches its destination (Albugmi et al., 2016) . Hence, it is especially important to protect data in transit with the help of encryption technologies. End-to-end encryption (E2EE) can help to mitigate the risk of data interception by encrypting the data at the point of origin and decrypting it at its destination (Troncoso, 2019). WhatsApp is currently using a comprehensive E2EE provided by the acquired company Whisper (Jon, 2014).

This shows that WhatsApp has invested heavily in the data confidentiality of their data in transit (messages) and “brought encryption to the masses” (Dwoskin, 2014).

However, WhatsApp still faces criticism for failing to deal with malware and viruses as well as privacy issues (Black, 2020). In conclusion, WhatsApp is taking steps and has invested towards ethical responsibility in terms of cyber security, but still has a long way to go.
