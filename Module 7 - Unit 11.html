Module 7 - Unit 11

The Expert Witness


This week’s learning explores concerns related to the digital expert witness. The learning should lead you towards the questioning of bias and accuracy, as well as expertise. You will look at some of the following aspects, implications and issues:

- Rules around expert evidence in different jurisdictions.
- Presenting your findings.
- Making complex data accessible to a lay audience.


On completion of this unit you will be able to:

- Explore issues concerning expert witness.
- Practice critical thinking skills.
- Practice research skills.


READING

The reading this week focusses on issues concerning the digital expert witness. The main issue that you will consider is the problem of standardisation of procedures and how these might affect outcomes of investigations. The recommended reading will help you in adding context to your final assessment due in week 12.


Required Reading

Karie, N., Kebande, V., Venter, H.S. & Choo, KK.R. (2019) On the importance of standardising the process of generating digital forensic reports. Forensic Science Internation 1.

Additional Reading

Ward, T. (2013) Expert evidence and the Law Commission : implementation without legislation? Criminal Law Review (7) 561-576

Ward, T. (2020) Explaining and trusting expert evidence: What is a ‘sufficiently reliable scientific basis’? The International Journal of Evidence & Proof 24(3) 233-254

Montasari, R., & Hill, R. (2019) Next-Generation Digital Forensics: Challenges and Future Paradigms. 2019 IEEE 12th International Conference on Global Security, Safety and Sustainability (ICGS3). London, UK, 2019. 205-212.

Shinder, D. (2010) Testifying as an expert witness in computer crime cases.
