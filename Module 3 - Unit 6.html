End of Module Refection: Being part of a development team

My experience of the Risk Management Module was one of particular interest because the module highlighted the establishment of risk as being unique to every business. Whist the ultimate goal (in this capacity) of every business remains the same to identifying risk, assess risk and reduce risk to an acceptable level (Stoneburner et al, 2002).

Our team project, a risk assessment, was based on the SME (Small/Medium Enterprise) business Acme Manufacturing (AM), who produce niche sporks. We were tasked with advising and assessing risk for three ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning System) solutions that the company would be potentially implementing. Ultimately, after assessing risk for our proposed solutions the recommendation was to go with a ‘Commercial Off the Shelf’ (COTS) solution. Some group members had ideas relating to hybrid infrastructure, however my position remained as that of Rhodes (2009), the future being in cloud computing. This COTS solution came with a higher level of initial investment but ultimately, would provide AM with the lowest risk factor. AM having their own inhouse IT team, would be capable of providing appropriate support, therefore requiring lower level of outsourced annual charges.

As a team we decided a ‘discord group’ to be the best form of communicating, sharing documents and discussing the various tasks required. This, paired with Google Docs enabled the sharing and editing of work needed for submission. Appendix A and B below show how our group arranged the various tasks required. We established which section we would like to undertake; segments were allocated based on individual strengths. Appendix C and D show my contributions to unit three and six respectively, I also assisted with structure, spelling and grammar amendments.

After some deliberation regarding which standards sould be used, our group settled on ‘The Open FAIR’ body of knowledge as the risk framework best suited the project. Josey et al (2014) state that “risk equals the probable frequency and probable magnitude of future loss” which was a deciding factor.

I consider that I participated well in the discussions, used knowledge gained from established work experience, assessed the risk, and supplied both meaningful comments and alternative suggestions. The module made me feel that risk management on a global scale should be implemented more widely. This group work was overall beneficial as it enabled me to better understand others thought processes. I was also able to both comprehend and propose different outcomes, leading to a more in-depth assessment report.

The geographical time difference between team members was challenging, I am based in the Caribbean (GMT-5) and others across, North America, Europe, and Asia. This certainly was not easy, nor ideal and made group calls almost impossible. Another challenge was navigating the UOE web platform which can be difficult, especially when searching for academic reading material and papers pertaining specific subject matter.

In conclusion, I learnt that discussing strengths, weaknesses, division of tasks and understanding frameworks is the best approach for a successful group outcome. Strong communication skills and planning ultimately make the end goal more achievable. I look forward to the projects upcoming and gaining greater knowledge of the complexities surrounding the world of cyber security.
