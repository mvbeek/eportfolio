Module 1 - Unit 3

Launching into Cyber Security May 2021

Collaborative Learning Discussion 1

Provide a summary of the discussion based on your initial post, the feedback from your peers and the content of all 3 units.

SUMMARY POST

My initial post highlighted the importance of cybersecurity globally due to the world's increased hyper-connectivity through the Internet and used Twitter as a case study example. This importance in conjunction with rapid web development still relies (at least in part) on dated, legacy systems that were designed decades ago leaving vulnerabilities that are difficult to navigate and challenging to mitigate (Tofan, 2018). As a result, cybersecurity is being placed front and center to protect these systems.

This notion can be further considered with some fantastic points made by my peers, particularly pertaining to the major social engineering hack on Twitter. Nils Linhoff comments that the twitter hackers received over 100,000 USD in a few hours (Whittaker, 2020), he also highlights that the attack was successful by using social engineering which is very much on the rise. I would agree that by introducing effective security awareness training this attack could have been mitigated or at the least had less impact by ensuring for example simply one user breach rather than multiple.

Considering how image can be impacted, Adrian Boscu rightly points out that it would seem Twitter's reputation was hardly affected by this breach as they still are a very popular social platform. Although this was arguably not a public relations disaster it would be interesting to establish how an attack of the same magnitude would have affected the reputation of a company within the financial services sector for example. Per Megan McClintock, it’s also important to consider the ownership and accountability issue. It would certainly be difficult to prove whether a user wrote a tweet coming from their account, however, political and societal consequences could have a huge effect publicly, especially where a tweet had the potential to cause mass hysteria or rioting.  

On a more technical level and of current relevance, particularly during the pandemic, we are currently experiencing, I also agree with Shota Kameyama regarding remote working and how important it is to implement an MDM solution (Stanko, T., Sena, J., Sena, M. 2016). This ensures devices get the critical updates and patches they require and also protects data if a device is stolen or lost.

As a result of the above, we can see the critical importance of including social engineering in any firm’s security awareness training (Eminağaoğlu, 2009). Private companies should certainly have some accountability when personal information is leaked and as countries are implementing national security standards these firms must prove that they are stepping in line. That being said, it’s important to remember that security standards and regulations will not necessarily prevent an attack but may reduce negative lasting effects.  

Considering what we have learned about international regulations such as GDPR, the CIA, and the Shell case study; it can be seen that the multi-faceted nature of any global company evidently provides multiple opportunities for hackers to infiltrate systems either for financial or political gain. The impact of this can be damage to the reputation of one’s company and is well worth an investment in security breach mitigation practices.
