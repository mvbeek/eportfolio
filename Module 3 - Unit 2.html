Module 3 - Unit 2

Information Risk Management 

Collaborative Learning Discussion 1

Read the Spears & Barki (2010) article (Unit 2 reading list) then discuss the following:

- How did the authors use both Qualitative and Quantitative assessment approaches? What benefits did each approach yield?
- What do the authors list as the advantages of involving users in the risk management process?
Based on the findings of the research,
 - How will the lack of user access affect the risk assessment you will carry out as part of your assessment?
 - Will it affect the choice of Qualitative vs. Quantitative assessment methods you utilise?
 - How might you mitigate any issues encountered?

READING

Sutton, D. (2014) Information Risk Management. 1st ed. Swindon, UK: BCS Learning & Development Limited.

Chapter 4.
Spears, J. & Barki, H. (2010) User Participation in Information Systems Security Risk Management. MIS Quarterly 34(3): 503.


INITIAL POST

Spears & Barki (2010) have used qualitative and quantitative assessment approaches by undertaking two primary research studies. The first, a qualitative assessment involved interviewing eleven participants across five organizations with the goal of understanding user participation within Information Security (IS) as part of Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) compliance. From the findings of this qualitative research, a framework was developed which included user participation theories in their system development literature.

The second study, a quantitative assessment, included 228 volunteers from the Information Systems Audit and Control Association (ISACA). The volunteers completed a survey, the results of which supported the aforementioned research framework.

Benefits of the qualitative assessment include, improved awareness of IS, control development and better business alignment. The benefits of their quantitative assessment being predominantly the reach they were able to achieve and in the dynamic spectrum of participants, this provided a high-quality level of data for analysis. One further benefit of the quantitative research undertaken, is the authors having been able to use this in order to corroborate the findings of their qualitative research, ensuring an accurate and well researched report.

Based on the findings of the report, it’s clear that the lack of user access will mean that as a group, in our own assignment, we will not be able to establish any primary data findings. Our group work will need to be based on either qualitative or quantitative secondary data, researched as thoroughly as possible. The data pool will be more limited than desired and our undertaken research will be more hypothetical then practical. To mitigate a weaker report quality, caused by lack of user access, our group will ensure to ascertain research based on similar companies to the one in question.

Having considered the Spears & Barki article in some detail, it’s viable to conclude that there is strength in the utilization of both qualitative and quantitative assessments. These used in conjunction with each other certainly lead to more accurate reporting, in turn that has increased user awareness and assisted in the alignment of business goals.

References:

Spears, J. L., & Barki, H. (2010). User Participation in Information Systems Security Risk Management. MIS Quarterly, 34(3), 503–522. https://doi.org/10.2307/25750689
