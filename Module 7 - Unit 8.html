Module 7 - Unit 8

Digital Deviance


This weekâ€™s learning explores a very difficult question: why individuals commit cyberspace crimes. This is an important question, because the answer(s) might affect the way this crime is dealt with. You will evaluate whether traditional criminological theories can explain this sort of deviance, or whether there is need to rethink our understanding of the reasons why people engage in cyber harmful activities.


On completion of this unit you will be able to:

- Explore issues concerning digital deviance.
- Assess the extent criminological theories can make sense of cyberspace deviance.
- Practice research skills.
- Apply evaluation skills.


READING

The reading this week focusses on issues concerning digital deviance. The reading considers the question of why individuals engage in cybercrime, and whether this engagement can be explained through criminological theories of crime.

Required Reading

Holt, T., Bossler, A. & Seigfried-Spellar, K. (2015) Cybercrime and Digital Forensics. London: Routledge.

Chapter 9


Additional Reading

Hinduja, S. & Patchin, J.W. (2008) Cyberbullying: An Exploratory Analysis of Factors Related to Offending and Victimization. Deviant Behavior 29(2): 129-156.
